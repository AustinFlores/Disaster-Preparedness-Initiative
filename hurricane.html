Hurricane Awareness

What is a Hurricane?

A hurricane is a type of tropical cyclone, a storm system characterized by a center of low pressure, a closed low-level circulation, and a spiral arrangement of thunderstorms. They can cause severe damage due to strong winds, heavy rainfall, and storm surges.

How to Prepare

Preparing for a hurricane involves having an emergency plan, assembling a disaster supply kit, and securing your property. Stay informed about the storm's progress through local news and weather services.

Safety Tips

  • Stay indoors and away from windows during the storm.
  • Have an emergency kit with food, water, and first aid supplies.
  • Evacuate if instructed by local authorities.
  • Listen to weather updates and follow emergency instructions.

FAQ

How long do hurricanes last?

Hurricanes can last anywhere from a few hours to over a week.

Can hurricanes be predicted?

Hurricanes can last anywhere from a few hours to over a week, depending on their path and the weather systems steering them. Even after the winds subside, the aftermath (flooding, damage) can last much longer.

What’s the difference between a hurricane, cyclone, and typhoon?

  • Hurricane: Atlantic and northeastern Pacific.
  • Typhoon: Northwestern Pacific.
  • Cyclone: South Pacific and Indian Oceans.

How does climate change affect hurricanes?

Climate change is believed to increase the intensity and frequency of strong hurricanes. Warmer ocean temperatures fuel more powerful storms, and rising sea levels can lead to more devastating storm surges.

What is the difference between a hurricane watch and a hurricane warning?

  • Hurricane Watch: Issued when hurricane conditions (sustained winds of 74 mph or higher) are possible in a specified area within 48 hours.
  • Hurricane Warning: Issued when hurricane conditions are expected in a specified area within 36 hours.
