EARTHQUAKE

What is an Earthquake?

An earthquake is what happens when two blocks of the earth suddenly slip past one another. The surface where they slip is called the fault or fault plane. The location below the earth’s surface where the earthquake starts is called the hypocenter, and the location directly above it on the surface of the earth is called the epicenter.

Earthquake Safety Guidelines

Before an Earthquake
  • Make sure you have a fire extinguisher, first aid kit, a battery-powered radio, a flashlight, and extra batteries at home.
  • Learn first aid.
  • Learn how to turn off the gas, water, and electricity.
  • Make up a plan of where to meet your family after an earthquake.
  • Don't leave heavy objects on shelves (they'll fall during a quake).
  • Anchor heavy furniture, cupboards, and appliances to the walls or floor.
  • Learn the earthquake plan at your school or workplace.
During an Earthquake
  • Stay calm! If you're indoors, stay inside. If you're outside, stay outside.
  • If you're indoors, stand against a wall near the center of the building, stand in a doorway, or crawl under heavy furniture. Stay away from windows and outside doors.
  • If you're outdoors, stay in the open away from power lines or anything that might fall.
  • Don't use matches, candles, or any flame. Broken gas lines and fire don't mix.
  • If you're in a car, stop the car and stay inside until the earthquake stops.
  • Don't use elevators (they may shut down).
After an Earthquake
  • Check yourself and others for injuries. Provide first aid for anyone who needs it.
  • Check water, gas, and electric lines for damage. If any are damaged, shut off the valves. Check for the smell of gas.
  • Turn on the radio. Don't use the phone unless it's an emergency.
  • Stay out of damaged buildings.
  • Be careful around broken glass and debris. Wear boots or sturdy shoes.
  • Stay away from beaches. Tsunamis and seiches sometimes hit after an earthquake.
  • Expect aftershocks.

Historical Earthquakes in the Philippines

The largest historical earthquake in the Philippines was the 1918 Celebes Sea earthquake, which measured 8.3 on the moment magnitude scale (Mw). Other major earthquakes in the Philippines include:

  • 1968 Casiguran Earthquake: This earthquake struck Casiguran, Aurora on August 2, 1968, and measured 7.3 on the moment magnitude scale. It killed 270 people and injured 261, and caused millions of dollars in property damage.
  • 1976 Moro Gulf Earthquake: This earthquake measured 8.0 on the moment magnitude scale.
  • 1990 Luzon Earthquake: This earthquake struck the northern and central parts of Luzon on July 16, 1990, and measured 7.8 on the moment magnitude scale. It killed more than 1,200 people and caused P10 billion in infrastructure damage.
  • 2002 Mindanao Earthquake: This earthquake measured 7.5 on the moment magnitude scale.

The Philippines is located where several tectonic plates interact, which can lead to major earthquakes.

What Causes Earthquakes?

Earthquakes are primarily caused by the movement of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's surface. The Earth's crust is divided into several large pieces, called tectonic plates, that float on the semi-fluid layer of the mantle. These plates are constantly moving, but they often become stuck at their edges due to friction. When the stress on the plates becomes too great, they suddenly slip or rupture, releasing a massive amount of energy in the form of seismic waves, causing an earthquake.

Earthquakes can also be caused by volcanic activity, where the movement of magma beneath the Earth's crust generates pressure that causes the surrounding rocks to crack. Additionally, human activities such as mining, reservoir-induced seismicity from large dams, and hydraulic fracturing (fracking) can induce seismic events, though these are generally smaller than natural tectonic earthquakes.

FAQS
  • What causes earthquakes? - Earthquakes are primarily caused by the movement of tectonic plates. They can also result from volcanic activity, landslides, or human activities.
  • What are tectonic plates? - Tectonic plates are large sections of the Earth’s crust that move slowly over the mantle. Their interactions lead to seismic activity.
  • What are the different types of earthquakes? - Tectonic, Volcanic, Collapse, and Explosion Earthquakes.
  • How is the magnitude of an earthquake measured? - Using the Richter scale or Moment Magnitude Scale (Mw).
  • What is the difference between magnitude and intensity? - Magnitude measures energy; intensity measures effects.
  • What are aftershocks? - Smaller quakes that follow the main earthquake.
